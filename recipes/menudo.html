Menudo

Description

This rich Pinoy pork menudo dish is usually prepared on special occasions or when you're expecting company.

Ingredients

  • 2 ¼ boneless pork chops
  • ½ pound pork liver
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 onion, diced
  • salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 tomatoes, diced
  • 1 (15.5 ounce) can garbanzo beans, drained
  • 1 (1.5 ounce) box raisins
  • 2 potatoes, diced

Steps

  1. Place pork chops in a large pot and cover with water. Bring to a boil and cook until pork is no longer pink in the center, about 20 minutes. Remove pork chops from pot and set aside to cool. Reserve water in pot.
  2. Place pork liver in a pot and cover with water. Bring to a boil and cook until liver is no longer pink in the center, about 20 minutes. Remove liver from pot and set aside to cool. Reserve water in pot.
  3. Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add garlic and onion; cook and stir until onion is translucent, about 5 minutes. Season with salt and pepper. Add tomatoes; cook and stir until soft, about 5 minutes.
  4. Peel and dice pork chops and liver. Add pork chops, liver, garbanzo beans, raisins, and potatoes to the skillet. Pour in reserved water from pork chops and liver. Bring to a boil; reduce heat and simmer until potatoes are tender, about 30 minutes.
